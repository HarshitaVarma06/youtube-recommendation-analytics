🔴Critical: Onboarding Failure
The platform retains only 25.7% of users after their first month, stabilising there. Users are arriving but not forming habits. This is an onboarding & first-session personalisation problem, not a content quality problem — content engagement metrics are strong.
🟡Funnel Breaks at Subscribe Step
30% of viewers like content, 10% comment — but only 5% subscribe. The gap between liking and subscribing is the platform's biggest lost value. A subscribe prompt triggered at the 70%+ watch mark could meaningfully close this gap.
🟢Content Quality is the Real Driver
Top-quality videos (watch % >0.75) drive a 62.2% completion rate vs 22.7% for low-quality videos — a 2.7× difference. Investing in content quality has a direct, measurable impact on every downstream metric.

🔴The Retention Cliff is an Onboarding Problem
After the month-1 drop, retention flatlines at ~26% through month 11. This is the hallmark of a cold-start failure — users are not being given a compelling reason to return in their first 30 days. Fixing month-1 retention from 25.7% → 35% would add ~9,300 recurring active users without acquiring anyone new.
🟡Q4 2023 Shows the Worst Bounce (5.4%) — Seasonal Risk
Bounce rate spikes in Q4 2023 to 5.4% — the platform's highest — then recovers. Q4 seasonality (competing for attention during holidays) depresses completion and engagement. Scheduling high-quality, shorter-form content in Q4 can help defend against annual engagement dips.
🟢51K+ Users Already Watch 75%+ of Every Video
51,362 users average over 75% watch depth per session. These are high-intent users who need a structured path to subscription — not more content. A targeted "subscribe" nudge for this cohort would convert at well above the current 5% platform average.

🌙Night Viewers Are Your Most Valuable Audience
Hours 20–2 AM show consistently the highest completion rates (55.1% at 20h, 55.0% at 2h). These users are in a lean-back, low-distraction mindset. Premium content drops at 20:00–21:00 will reach the most engaged cohort.
📅Friday Converts Best — Plan Releases Accordingly
Friday has the highest engagement score (0.3455) and subscription rate (5.1%) of any day. This is when users are most likely to turn a passive watch into an active subscription. Major content drops should be scheduled for Thursday evening or Friday.
📱Device Parity is a Product Success — Not a Neutral Signal
All four devices show nearly identical engagement (~0.344). This uniformity means the product experience is platform-agnostic — a meaningful engineering and UX achievement. The only opportunity is Tablet's marginally higher watch depth (74.9%), suggesting screen size still matters at the margin.

🎯Medium is the Biggest Growth Lever
53,710 users are in Medium — the platform majority. Their conversion rate (3.8%) already generates real volume. Moving just 10% of them to High adds ~5,400 high-value users. This cohort needs nudges toward deeper content — not new users.
👑Power Users Convert at 2.4× Platform Rate
Power users achieve 11.8% conversion vs 5% platform average. Their like rate (67.7%) is over 3× the platform average. These users have found their niche — the product should identify what that niche is and use it to personalise the journey for Medium/High users.
⚠️Low Engagers Watch Less Often, Not Worse
Low segment watches only 6.6 videos on average — frequency is the problem, not depth. They don't have a habit yet. A push notification strategy (personalised digest of new content in their viewed categories) is the right intervention, not more content volume.

📚Education is the Platform's Retention Engine
Education leads on completion (54.9%) and like rate (30.2%). Educational viewers have intent — they finish what they start. Long-form series with structured curricula will outperform standalone videos in this category. Education is also where Creator investment has the clearest ROI.
📰News Needs Format Reform
News ranks last on completion (54.5%), like rate (29.9%), and bounce (5.0%). News consumption is fundamentally transactional — users scan, extract value, leave. Pivoting to 2–4 minute digest formats with strong visual packaging will better match this behavior pattern.
🎮Gaming & Sports Subscribe Fast but Don't Always Finish
Gaming (5.1% sub rate) and Sports (5.1%) lead on conversions but don't top completion. Users subscribe to channels based on trailers, highlights, or brand — not by completing a full video. Clip-first content strategies and strong channel branding are more important here than runtime.

🏆Top Videos Serve 50.4% of All Views
504,021 views come from Top-quality videos — the majority of all traffic. The platform's recommendation algorithm is already directing users toward the best content. The opportunity is to expand this top tier by raising the quality floor across Medium and High buckets.
🗑️Low-Quality Videos Should Be Deprioritised
Low-quality videos account for just 1,274 views but drive 17% bounce rates — 5.5× the top-quality rate. They actively harm the session experience. The recommendation engine should aggressively reduce their distribution until quality benchmarks improve.
📈Quality Improvement Has Direct Revenue Impact
Moving a video from High → Top tier increases completion by 12.9 percentage points. At platform scale, if 10% of High-quality videos improved to Top, that represents ~5.2M additional completed views per year — each a subscription opportunity.

01
Fix Month-1 Onboarding — The Single Highest-ROI Action
74.3% of users never return after their first month. Implement a personalised first-30-day journey: daily content digest emails, in-app "because you watched" recommendations seeded from their first session, and a milestone notification ("You've watched 5 videos — here's what similar viewers loved"). Target: lift month-1 retention from 25.7% to 35%. That's 9,300 additional recurring monthly active users from the existing user base.
📊 Impact: +9,300 retained users ⚡ Effort: Medium 🎯 Priority: #1 Critical
02
Place Subscribe CTA at the 70% Watch Mark — Not the End
30% of viewers like content but only 5% subscribe — a 94% funnel drop. Most subscribe prompts appear at the end of a video, after the highest-intent moment has passed. A/B test an in-video subscribe nudge triggered when a user crosses 70% completion (the moment intent is highest). A 2pp lift in conversion rate adds ~20,000 new subscribers across the existing user base with no new content spend.
📊 Impact: +20,000 subscribers ⚡ Effort: Low 🎯 Priority: #2 High
03
Deprioritise Low-Quality Videos in Recommendations
Low-quality videos (avg watch % <50%) generate a 17% bounce rate — 5.5× higher than top-quality videos. They consume recommendation slots, damage user experience, and waste session time. Update the recommendation algorithm to apply a quality score penalty for videos below the 50% watch-depth threshold. This is a recommendation tuning change, not a content moderation decision. Reducing Low-quality exposure by 80% saves ~216 wasted sessions per 1,000 recommendations.
📊 Impact: −5.5× bounce in served content ⚡ Effort: Low 🎯 Priority: #3 High
04
Build a "High → Power" Upgrade Path for 44,390 High Engagers
High engagers convert at 6.4% — Power users at 11.8%. The behavioral gap is watch frequency and habit formation, not content quality preference. A streak-based loyalty program (watching 5 days in a row unlocks early access, custom categories, or creator AMAs) gives high engagers a reason to build a daily habit. Converting 5% of High → Power adds 2,200 users at Power-level LTV (11.8% conversion, 67.7% like rate).
📊 Impact: +2,200 Power-tier users ⚡ Effort: High 🎯 Priority: #4 Medium
05
Reformat News Content + Schedule Releases on Friday 20:00
News has the platform's worst completion (54.5%) and like rate (29.9%). Pivot to 2–4 minute news digest formats with visual summaries — matching the scan-and-leave behavior of News consumers. Separately, schedule all major content releases for Friday 20:00: the combination of the highest-engagement day and the highest-completion hour. This affects all categories and costs zero additional content spend.
📊 Impact: +Completion & sub rate across releases ⚡ Effort: Low 🎯 Priority: #5 Medium
