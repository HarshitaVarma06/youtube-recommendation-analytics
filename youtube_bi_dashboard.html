Sticky content: users watch 3/4 of every video
An average watch depth of 74.8% per user signals strong content relevance. The majority of the audience is not grazing — they're committing. This reduces ad-drop-off risk and is a positive signal for algorithmic ranking.
30% of users show churn-risk patterns
30,156 users have never converted (subscribed/liked/commented) AND watch below the platform median. Without re-engagement they represent the highest near-term churn risk. This cohort warrants a dedicated re-targeting effort.
Conversion funnel has a 94% drop at the subscribe step
Only 5% of views lead to a subscription. While watching → liking (30%) is healthy, the jump from liking to subscribing is where most value is lost. Optimizing the subscribe CTA placement is the single highest-leverage retention play.

Tablets lead in watch depth & completion
Tablet users average 74.9% watch depth and the highest completion rate (54.8%). Larger screen, leisure posture. This is the "lean back" device — scheduling high-value content releases for tablet peak hours could amplify engagement.
5 AM and 5 PM are engagement dips
Hours 5 and 17 consistently show the lowest completion rates across the day. These are transition moments (morning rush, commute end) where users are time-pressured. Shorter-form content or series teasers may perform better in those slots.
Watch behavior is strikingly uniform across devices
All four devices (TV, Mobile, Desktop, Tablet) show near-identical engagement scores (~0.344). This means the product experience translates well across form factors — no device is being underserved or over-performing to a degree that demands immediate UX intervention.

Low engagers watch fewer videos, not worse
The Low segment watches only 6.6 videos on average — the same as Power users. Volume isn't their problem; depth is. A personalized re-engagement sequence targeting inactive sessions (not missing users) could lift them to Medium.
Power users convert at 2× the platform rate
At 11.8% conversion, Power users are your most valuable cohort — but at only 928 people, they're barely 1% of the base. There's a clear lever: identify what content High engagers (44K users) are missing to push them into Power. The gap is a 5.4pp conversion jump.
Medium is the platform's biggest growth lever
53,710 users sit in Medium — the majority cohort. Their conversion rate (3.8%) is already meaningful. Moving just 10% of them to High would add ~5,000 high-value users without acquiring a single new one.

Education completes highest — users come to learn
Education leads with a 54.9% completion rate. Users seeking to learn are inherently goal-oriented — they stay until they've got what they came for. This category also drives strong like rates (30.2%). Investing in educational content depth pays both retention and engagement dividends.
Gaming & Sports drive subscriptions but not completions
Gaming (5.1%) and Sports (5.1%) lead on subscription rate but rank below average on completion. Users subscribe after trailers or highlights without finishing the full video — an important distinction when measuring the funnel. Clip-first content strategies suit this behavior.
News is the weakest retention category
News ranks last on both completion (54.5%) and like rate (29.9%). News is consumed transactionally — users scan, extract, leave. Bundling news into short-form digest formats or push-notification summaries could increase stickiness without forcing longer viewing sessions.
Strategic Recommendations
4 Concrete Actions Tied to the Data
Prioritized by impact and effort based on the patterns identified across all 1M events
01
Launch a churn-prevention campaign for the 30,156 at-risk users.
These users have zero conversion events AND below-median watch depth. A targeted re-engagement email or in-app nudge with personalized "you might like" content (based on their category history) should aim to increase average watch % by 10pp. Even moving 15% of them to Moderate status adds 4,500 retained users at zero acquisition cost.
02
Optimize the subscribe CTA placement — the funnel drops 94% between like and subscribe.
30% of viewers like content but only 5% subscribe. A/B test an in-video subscribe prompt triggered at the 70% watch mark (when intent is highest) vs end-of-video. Even a 2pp lift in conversion rate would add ~20,000 net-new subscribers across the existing user base.
03
Build a "High → Power" upgrade path for the 44,390 High engagers.
High engagers already convert at 6.4% and watch 79.5% of videos. Power users convert at 11.8%. The behavioral gap is narrow: more consistent viewing sessions and slightly longer depth. A loyalty program (streaks, early access, exclusive content) targeting this cohort could shift 5–10% of them into Power tier, significantly boosting LTV.
04
Reformulate News content for scan-and-leave behavior, invest in Education depth.
News has the platform's lowest completion and like rates. Pivot to 2–5 minute digest formats with strong thumbnails optimized for click-through (current CTR: 83.3%). Simultaneously, double down on long-form Education content — it has the best completion rate (54.9%) and users are primed to subscribe after completing educational series. Education is the clearest watch-time compounder on the platform.
